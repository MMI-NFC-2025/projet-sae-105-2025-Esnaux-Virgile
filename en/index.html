Home page of Ada Lovelace

“ This brain that is mine is something more than merely mortal, as time will show. ”

Quote from Ada Lovelace

Women have always played a crucial role in science, yet their contributions have frequently been minimized, forgotten, or wrongly attributed to male colleagues. This historical injustice, still perceptible today, fuels a persistent underrepresentation of women in research and in the media coverage of their discoveries.

Among these forgotten figures, Ada Lovelace stands out as a major pioneer. Born in 1815, she is the daughter of the famous poet Lord Byron and a mother passionate about mathematics, who instills in her an early taste for the sciences. Ada Lovelace trains with great mathematicians of her time, including Charles Babbage, inventor of the analytical engine, an ancestor of the modern computer.
