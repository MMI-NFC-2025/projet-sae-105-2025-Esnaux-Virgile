What is the connection between the film “The Imitation Game” and Ada Lovelace?

Film Overview

The film “The Imitation Game” retraces the life of Alan Turing, a pioneer of computing, influenced by the work of Ada Lovelace. Ada Lovelace is cited in connection with the concept of artificial intelligence and the famous “Lovelace’s objection”, where she stated that machines cannot think for themselves, which inspired Turing to formulate the test of the same name. Their connection symbolizes two visions: Lovelace anticipated human-machine collaboration as a creative partnership, while Turing imagined the moment when artificial intelligence could rival humans.

"A machine can do what it is programmed to do, but that does not mean it cannot surprise us."

Quote from Alan Turing

What is the connection between Ada and this film?

In the film “The Imitation Game”, Alan Turing works with Joan Clarke, a brilliant mathematician who helps him decipher messages encoded by the German Enigma machine. Their collaboration is crucial to the project's success and strongly recalls that between Charles Babbage and Ada Lovelace, a century earlier, when they worked together on the analytical engine, a precursor to the computer.
